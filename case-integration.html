Case Study — Expense Integration Rollout

Finance alignment, integration readiness/testing, and post-launch reconciliation monitoring.

All content included here is sanitized, anonymized, and non-confidential.

Problem

Finance teams needed clean expense reconciliation and fewer manual workflows. The primary risk was misalignment on what “done” meant (technical completion vs. operational reconciliation accuracy).

What I Did

  • Aligned finance stakeholders on success criteria: defined what correct reporting/reconciliation should look like post-launch.
  • Coordinated integration readiness: confirmed data requirements, ownership, and testing approach with cross-functional partners.
  • Supported testing and validation: validated end-to-end flows and documented outcomes/edge cases for go-live confidence.
  • Monitored post-launch reconciliation: tracked early signals, identified exceptions, and drove corrective actions quickly.

Outcome

  • Improved reconciliation clarity and reduced post-launch confusion.
  • Reduced manual follow-ups by documenting ownership and exception paths.

Learning

  • Integrations succeed when finance definitions (what “correct” looks like) are locked before technical testing starts.
